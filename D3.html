Feeding mechanism in animals include; absorbing, biting and chewing, sucking, grinding and trapping and absorbing mechanism.
  1. Absorbing mechanism: The tapeworm is an endoparasite that feeds using the absorbing mechanism. They live and feed from the host using their hooks and suckers.
  2. Biting and chewing mechanism: Animals like grasshopper or cockroach have mouth parts adapted for biting and chewing. These parts include the labrum (upper lips), mandibles, maxillae and labium (lower lips)
  3. Sucking Mechanism: There are three popular organisms which exhibits sucking mechanism. These organisms are mosquito, butterfly and housefly. The insects have different modifications of mouth parts adapted for feeding on food through the mechanism of sucking.
  4. Grinding mechanism: Grinding mechanism is common among mammals e.g. man, goat, sheep, lion etc. these animals are capable of grinding the food before swallowing. The grinding of food is aided by the presence of hard and strong teeth made of enamel and dentine. The animals are adapted to the grinding mechanisms by the following;
    • They possess different types or sets of teeth
    • The teeth are strong and hard to withstand biting, chewing or grinding and cracking of solid food like bones
    • The possess incisors which have sharp and flat edged used for biting off bits of food
    • Animals have pointed canine teeth used for tearing solid food
    • There is the presence of pre-molars and molars with undulating and wide surface to enable grinding of food
    • The absence of front teeth or incisors in herbivores like sheep is a special adaptation as it helps to grip the grasses during feeding by the animal.
  5. The trapping and absorbing mechanism: this is common among insectivorous or carnivorous plants such as bladderwort and sundew. The bladderwort and sundew have structures which enable them to adapt to this mode of feeding or nutrition.
