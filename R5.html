CONDITIONS NECESSARY FOR RESPIRATION

For respiration (gaseous exchange) to effectively take place, the following conditions must be available;

  1. Respiratory medium: Respiratory medium refers to the surroundings of the organism from which it obtains oxygen. Examples of respiratory media are air and water
  2. Respiratory organ:This is the organ such as lungs in mammals and gills in fish through which oxygen is taken into the body and carbon dioxide and water vapour are given out. The taking in of one gas example oxygen and the given out of another example carbon dioxide and water vapour is called gaseous exchange.
  3. Transport medium: This is a system which moves dissolved gases example oxygen around the body to all cells and collect the gases example carbon dioxide which needs to be eliminated from the body. Example of transport medium in mammal is the blood.
  4. Respiratory surface: The respiratory surface refers to the definite surface of the body such as the surface of the lungs or a gill where gaseous exchange takes place.
  5. Ventilation: The movement of water or air in one direction over the respiratory surface is called ventilation. This helps to renew the air or water in contact with or near a respiratory surface. Ventilation mechanism as in human breathing, increases the rate of gaseous exchange
